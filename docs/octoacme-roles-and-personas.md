# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate the release process from code freeze through deployment and post-release verification. They manage deployment checklists, communications with stakeholders, and ensure smooth handoffs between development, QA, and operations teams.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain release schedules and deployment checklists
- Coordinate code freeze, branching, and release candidate preparation
- Facilitate go/no-go decisions with Product Managers and QA
- Manage deployment communications to stakeholders and support teams
- Oversee post-deployment verification and rollback procedures
- Track and communicate release metrics and issues

### Goals
- Deliver predictable, low-risk releases
- Minimize deployment-related incidents and rollbacks
- Maintain clear communication and transparency throughout release cycle
- Continuously improve release processes based on retrospectives

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness reviews with QA and engineering leads
- Deployment status updates to stakeholders and support teams
- Release notes and change summaries
- Post-mortem reports for failed or problematic releases

### Example Interactions
- Works with Product Manager to define release scope and timing
- Coordinates with QA to ensure all acceptance criteria are met before release
- Communicates deployment windows to Customer Success and support teams
- Collaborates with Developers on rollback plans and mitigation strategies

---

## Customer Success Manager

### Role Summary
Customer Success Managers (CSMs) act as the voice of the customer within the product team. They bring user feedback, feature requests, and production issues to Product Managers and engineering, and close the loop by ensuring customers are informed of fixes and improvements.

### Responsibilities
- Collect and prioritize customer feedback and feature requests
- Report production issues and usability problems from customers
- Collaborate with Product Managers on roadmap prioritization based on customer impact
- Communicate product updates and release notes to customers
- Validate that customer-reported issues are resolved
- Track customer satisfaction metrics and trends

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and retention
- Ensure customer needs inform product priorities
- Reduce time-to-resolution for customer-reported issues
- Build strong relationships between customers and the product team

### Typical Communication
- Weekly sync with Product Manager on customer priorities and feedback
- Customer escalations and priority issues to engineering
- Release announcements and feature education to customers
- Feedback reports and satisfaction trends to leadership

### Example Interactions
- Brings critical customer feedback to Product Manager for backlog prioritization
- Escalates production issues to Project Manager and engineering team
- Partners with Release Manager to communicate deployment windows to affected customers
- Works with UX Designer to validate that solutions address user pain points

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business stakeholders and the delivery team. They clarify business requirements, translate them into actionable user stories and acceptance criteria, and ensure that what gets built aligns with business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Translate business needs into detailed user stories and acceptance criteria
- Collaborate with Product Manager on backlog refinement and prioritization
- Work with Developers to clarify requirements and edge cases
- Validate that delivered features meet business requirements
- Document business processes and workflows

### Goals
- Ensure clear, complete, and testable requirements
- Reduce ambiguity and rework due to unclear specifications
- Align technical solutions with business value
- Facilitate efficient communication between business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Requirements gathering sessions with business stakeholders
- Backlog refinement meetings with Product Manager and Developers
- User story clarification and acceptance criteria reviews
- Business process documentation and workflow diagrams

### Example Interactions
- Works with Product Manager to break down epics into user stories with clear acceptance criteria
- Collaborates with Developers during sprint planning to clarify edge cases and dependencies
- Partners with Customer Success Manager to understand customer workflows and pain points
- Validates completed features against business requirements with QA team

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers drive user experience research, design, and validation. They create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications that guide implementation, and partner closely with Product Managers, Developers, and Customer Success to ensure usability and customer satisfaction.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Define interaction patterns and visual design specifications
- Collaborate with Developers on implementation feasibility and details
- Validate implemented features against design specifications
- Maintain design system and UI component libraries

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and improve task completion rates
- Ensure design consistency across the product
- Validate design decisions through user research and data

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Manager and stakeholders
- Implementation collaboration with Developers
- Usability testing sessions with customers (via Customer Success)
- Design specifications and component documentation

### Example Interactions
- Collaborates with Product Manager to understand user needs and define design requirements
- Works with Business Analyst to understand workflows and create optimal user journeys
- Partners with Developers to ensure designs are technically feasible and implemented accurately
- Shares usability findings with Customer Success Manager to validate customer pain points
